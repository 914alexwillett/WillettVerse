Economic Learning App Part 2

I voiced to the App my concerns for non-linearity and trying to ground things more in statistics over saying X causes Y. It stimulated an interesting point of framing things in terms of economic regimes as opposed to hard and fast rules that stay static. 

"""
Markets are conditional Systems, inflation rising does NOT mean `Stocks Down.` 
It means: 
- `Stocks down IF liquidity is tightening`
- `Stocks go up IF growth is accelerating faster than inflation`
- `Neutral IF inflation is already priced`
"""

It claims that my app is becoming a macro systems laboratory. So it's not a basic dashboard, it's not a trading tool. Instead we're looking at aspects like conditional relationships, structural shifts, interaction effects, variance drivers.

It warned my about the short comings of using ANOVA and Correlation with such a small sample size, history only happened once (that we are aware of). To auto generate regimes, rather than me manually coming up with them. This would use K-means clusters of various data points. 

K-means clustering as a refresher takes multiple variables, groups time periods on similarity, and finds clusters of macro conditions. "The algorithm says: These periods are statistically similar across variables."
